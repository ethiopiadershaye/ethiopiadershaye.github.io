Project Idea

This will be an app compatible with both android and iOS, which acts primarily as a directory app for shopping centres such as Highpoint, Melbourne Central, Emporium Melbourne and Chadstone. It will be fitted with shops such as Factorie, Adidas and Target as well as the different restaurants available in the food court including places like McDonald’s, Dumplings Plus and Max Brenner.

According to worldatlas.com, of the largest shopping centres in Australia, Victoria is leading with Chadstone Shopping Centre as number 1. Victoria also home to 4 of the top 10 biggest shopping centres with New South Wales and Queensland close behind at 3 each. Chadstone Shopping Centre is 190,000 square metres, which means that it’s almost the size of 11 MCG’s! Remembering where each and every store is located and the way to get there can be challenging, especially if you aren’t regularly going shopping in the same shopping centres. Therefore, an app that gets you to your favourite shops is long overdue.

The apps main feature will be its ability to direct users to and from their favourite stores. Individuals will be able to choose from categories such as clothing, accessories, shoes and cafes and restaurants or simply search their desired store from a list of all available stores. Once individuals have selected a store, they will be directed with step-by-step instructions on how to get there. Users will also have the ability to create a sequence of stores, which the app will scan and find the closest store to the individual. The app will then make its way down the list in order of store proximity. Individuals will also be able to search for the nearest toilets based on their location. Additionally included will be a deals and discounts page where you can see store offers before getting to the store to assist in deciding whether or not you would like to shop there. In addition to these features, a Favourites tab will also be implemented to save time searching for stores, where individuals will be able to favourite stores they frequently visit to add to their list of stores they would like to visit. Another feature of the app will be the ability to find where there are empty parking spaces to spend less time finding car parking and more time shopping. After the app user has found a parking space, they will be able to drop a pin where the car was parked to assist in easier access to it, once they have completed their shop. The app will store where the car was parked and when the individual is ready to leave, give directions from their current location, straight to their car using the exit closest to where their car was parked. They will also be able to have the option to set a timer if the parking space has a time limit. A reminder will be given in the last 15 mins and again in the last 5 mins remaining.

If this project were successful, it would become extremely helpful and essential to the shopping experience, putting more time back into shopping. This app makes going shopping more convenient by eliminating those factors that steer us away from shopping at particular places, such as not finding parking. Also, rather than getting lost and looking for directory maps or information desks around the shopping centre, or remembering where you parked your car hours and hours ago, you can focus on what you went there to do, shop.
